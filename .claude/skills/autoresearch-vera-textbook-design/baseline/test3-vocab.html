Economics & Finance Vocabulary · 핵심 10선

01 austerity [ɔːstérəti] n.

긴축 (재정), 검소함

The government imposed austerity measures to reduce the deficit.

= frugality, stringency, belt-tightening

02 liquidity [likwídəti] n.

유동성, 현금화 가능성

Banks must maintain adequate liquidity to meet customer withdrawals.

= fluidity, solvency, cash availability

03 deflation [difléiʃən] n.

디플레이션, 물가 하락

Prolonged deflation can lead to a sharp decline in consumer spending.

= price decline, contraction ↔ inflation

04 collateral [kəlǽtərəl] n. / adj.

담보 (물); 부수적인

The bank required property as collateral for the loan.

= security, pledge, guarantee

05 recession [riséʃən] n.

경기 침체, 불황

The economy slipped into a recession after two consecutive quarters of negative growth.

= downturn, slump, contraction

06 arbitrage [άːrbitrɑːʒ] n.

차익 거래, 재정 거래

Traders exploit price differences through arbitrage across international markets.

= price differential trading, spread trading

07 depreciation [dipriːʃiéiʃən] n.

감가상각; (화폐) 가치 하락

Annual depreciation of the equipment was recorded as a non-cash expense.

= devaluation, amortization, write-down

08 fiscal [fískəl] adj.

재정의, 국고의

The new fiscal policy aimed to stimulate growth through tax cuts.

= financial, budgetary, governmental

09 dividend [dívidènd] n.

배당금; 이익, 보상

Shareholders received a quarterly dividend of $2.50 per share.

= distribution, payout, return

10 leverage [lévrɪdʒ] n. / v.

레버리지, 부채 활용; 활용하다

The firm used financial leverage to amplify its return on equity.

= borrowing power, gearing, influence

Key Point — 혼동 주의

deflation (물가 하락) vs. depreciation (자산·화폐 가치 하락) — 둘 다 '하락'이지만 대상이 다르다.

fiscal (재정의, 정부 예산) vs. monetary (통화의, 중앙은행) — 경제 정책 문제에서 자주 구분 출제됨.
