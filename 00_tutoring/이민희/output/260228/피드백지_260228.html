88번 — 세부 정보 파악

Every year, millions of monarch butterflies make one of nature's most incredible journeys. These delicate insects travel up to 3,000 miles from Canada and the United States to central Mexico. What makes this journey even more amazing is that the butterflies that start the trip are not the same ones that finish it. The journey takes multiple generations. The butterflies that begin the trip in the fall will mate and die along the way. Their children and grandchildren continue the journey. Even more mysterious is how they know where to go. Scientists believe they use the sun, magnetic fields, and even genetic memory to navigate. The butterflies that reach Mexico will spend the winter there, clustered together on trees to stay warm. In the spring, they begin the journey back north, but again, it takes several generations to complete the round trip.

91번 — 세부 정보 파악

Origami, the Japanese art of paper folding, is much more than just a hobby. The word "origami" comes from two Japanese words: "ori" meaning "to fold" and "kami" meaning "paper." This ancient art form requires only one material: a square piece of paper. No scissors, glue, or tape are allowed. Despite this limitation, origami artists can create incredibly complex and beautiful sculptures. From simple cranes and flowers to detailed dragons and geometric shapes, the possibilities are endless. Origami teaches important life skills. It requires precision, patience, and the ability to follow detailed instructions. It also helps develop spatial reasoning and fine motor skills. Many schools now use origami in math classes because it helps students understand geometry and fractions. The meditative nature of folding paper can also reduce stress and improve focus. What starts as a flat piece of paper becomes a three-dimensional work of art through the magic of folding.

97번 — 세부 정보 파악

One of America's favorite treats, the chocolate chip cookie, was actually invented by accident. In 1938, Ruth Wakefield was running the Toll House Inn in Massachusetts. She was preparing chocolate cookies for her guests when she realized she was out of baker's chocolate. Instead, she broke up a bar of semi-sweet chocolate and added the pieces to her cookie dough, expecting them to melt and create chocolate cookies. However, the chocolate pieces held their shape, creating the world's first chocolate chip cookies. The guests loved them so much that Ruth began serving them regularly. Word spread about these amazing cookies, and soon people were asking for the recipe. Ruth eventually sold the recipe to Nestlé chocolate company in exchange for a lifetime supply of chocolate. The Toll House Chocolate Chip Cookie became so popular that it is now considered an American classic. Today, billions of chocolate chip cookies are eaten around the world every year.

Ruth는 분명한 기대(녹아서 초콜릿 쿠키가 되기를)를 가지고 있었으므로, '아무것도 기대하지 않았다'는 사실과 반대.
학생 답 분석

어디서 막혔을까: 지문에서 초콜릿 조각이 '형태를 유지했다(held their shape)'는 결과를 읽고, 이것을 Ruth의 '기대'로 착각한 것으로 추정. 실제로는 기대와 반대되는 결과가 나온 것인데, expect(기대)와 result(결과)의 시점 차이를 인식하지 못함.

오류 패턴: 기대/결과 혼동 — expecting(기대)과 However 이후의 actual result(실제 결과)를 구분하지 못함

집중 포인트: 'expecting/hoped/planned to...' 구문 뒤에 나오는 내용이 기대이고, 'However/But/Instead' 뒤에 나오는 내용이 실제 결과. 이 두 신호어를 짝으로 기억할 것. 지문을 읽을 때 expect → but/however 패턴을 만나면 '기대≠결과' 표시를 하며 읽기.
