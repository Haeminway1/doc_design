61번 — NOT 유형 (언급되지 않은 것)

The application of 3D printing technology in the medical field is revolutionizing patient care, particularly in the creation of custom implants and prosthetics. Traditionally, medical implants are mass-produced in standard sizes, which may not perfectly fit every patient's unique anatomy. This can lead to complications and longer recovery periods. However, 3D printing, also known as additive manufacturing, allows for the creation of patient-specific implants using data from CT scans or MRIs. Materials like titanium and biocompatible polymers are used to print everything from dental crowns to entire hip joints. This personalized approach ensures a perfect fit, which can significantly improve surgical outcomes, reduce the time a patient spends in surgery, and accelerate the healing process. The ability to create complex, porous structures also encourages better integration with the patient's own bone and tissue, promoting long-term stability and function.

① They are made from a wider variety of materials.
② They can be produced more quickly in large quantities.
③ They are designed to fit a patient's specific anatomy.
④ They are significantly less expensive to manufacture.
⑤ They completely eliminate the need for surgery.

62번 — 세부 정보 파악

To safeguard global food security and preserve agricultural biodiversity, seed banks have been established around the world. These facilities are designed for the long-term storage of seeds from a vast array of plant species. One of the most famous is the Svalbard Global Seed Vault in Norway, often called the "doomsday vault." It acts as a final backup for other seed banks globally. The vault is built deep inside a mountain in the Arctic, providing natural freezing conditions that are ideal for preserving seeds for centuries, or even millennia. By storing duplicates of seed samples from various national and regional gene banks, the Svalbard vault ensures that the genetic material of important food crops is not lost in the event of regional or global crises, such as natural disasters, war, or disease. This genetic diversity is crucial for developing new crop varieties that can adapt to changing climates and resist new pests.

64번 — 핵심 목적 파악

Green marketing is a business strategy that involves promoting products or services based on their environmental benefits. The goal is to appeal to a growing segment of consumers who are environmentally conscious. This can involve modifying products to be more eco-friendly, changing production processes to reduce waste, or adopting sustainable packaging. However, the rise of green marketing has also led to a deceptive practice known as "greenwashing." Greenwashing occurs when a company spends more time and money marketing itself as environmentally friendly than it does on minimizing its environmental impact. Companies might use vague terms like "eco-friendly" or "natural" without providing evidence, or highlight one small green initiative to distract from larger, more harmful practices. This can mislead consumers who genuinely want to make sustainable choices and can damage the credibility of legitimate green marketing efforts. Therefore, it is crucial for consumers to be critical and look for specific certifications or data to verify a company's claims.

오류 패턴: 키워드 매칭 함정 — '환경(environmental)'이라는 공통 키워드에 이끌려 선지를 선택. 지문의 실제 목적('appeal to consumers')을 놓침

집중 포인트: '목적(purpose/goal)' 문제는 지문에서 'The goal is...', 'The purpose of...', 'aims to...' 등의 목적 표현을 직접 찾아야 한다. 주제 키워드가 같다고 답이 되는 것이 아니다.
