Émile Durkheim's concept of "anomie" is a cornerstone of sociological theory, describing a condition of normlessness that leads to social pathology. ( ① ) In his study *Suicide*, Durkheim observed that suicide rates were not constant but varied with social conditions, and notably, they increased during periods of both economic depression and rapid economic prosperity. ( ② ) The link to depression seems intuitive, but the connection to prosperity requires a deeper explanation. ( ③ ) Durkheim's explanation lies in the role of society in regulating human desires, which are naturally insatiable. ( ④ ) When society is stable, it provides a moral framework that sets achievable goals and limits aspirations to a level that can be satisfied. ( ⑤ ) This painful disjunction between boundless desire and the limited means of satisfaction generates feelings of frustration, futility, and ultimately, a higher propensity for suicide.
