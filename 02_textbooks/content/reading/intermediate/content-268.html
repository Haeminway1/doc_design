The "Baader-Meinhof phenomenon," or frequency illusion, is the cognitive bias that occurs when, after noticing something for the first time, one has a tendency to notice it more often, leading to the belief that it has an increased frequency of occurrence. This illusion is the result of two psychological processes working together. The first is selective attention: once your brain is primed to a new word or concept, it is more likely to pick it out from your environment. The second is confirmation bias: each new sighting is interpreted as further proof of the object's sudden ubiquity. ________________________________________. The frequency of the thing you noticed has not actually changed; only your ability to perceive it has, creating a subjective and often amusing illusion.
