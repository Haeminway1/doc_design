The "categorical imperative" is the foundational principle of the deontological ethics of Immanuel Kant. It is a command of reason that is unconditional and binding on all rational agents, irrespective of their desires or goals. Kant's most famous formulation is the "formula of universal law": Act only according to that maxim whereby you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law without contradiction. This means the test of a moral action is not its consequences, but its logical universalizability. For example, a maxim of making a false promise to get a loan cannot be universalized. If everyone made false promises, the very institution of promising would collapse, and the maxim would become self-defeating. ________________________________________. It is a system grounded in duty and the internal consistency of the will, not in the pursuit of happiness or good outcomes.
