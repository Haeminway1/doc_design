The "iron cage" is a sociological concept from Max Weber used to describe the increased rationalization and bureaucratization of modern society. Weber argued that while these rational systems are highly efficient, they trap individuals in an impersonal, rule-governed reality that limits human freedom and creativity. (A) Social action becomes increasingly based on efficiency and calculation rather than on traditional values or emotions. (B) The pursuit of wealth, for example, becomes a purely mechanical activity, stripped of the ethical significance it may have once held. (C) This process leads to a profound "disenchantment of the world," where life is devoid of mystery and spiritual meaning. Weber was deeply ambivalent about this development, recognizing the material benefits of rationalization while lamenting its cost to the human spirit.
