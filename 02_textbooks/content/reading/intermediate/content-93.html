The economic concept of "creative destruction," coined by Joseph Schumpeter, describes the essential dynamic of capitalism. Schumpeter argued that the engine of economic progress is not price competition but innovation. This process of innovation incessantly revolutionizes the economic structure from within, destroying the old one and creating a new one. It is the turbulent but vital process that keeps the capitalist system dynamic.

The "Gaia hypothesis," formulated by James Lovelock and Lynn Margulis, proposes that the Earth's biosphere, atmosphere, oceans, and soil constitute a complex, self-regulating system. This system, named Gaia after the Greek goddess of the Earth, actively maintains the planetary conditions necessary for life. It is not that life simply adapts to a passive environment; rather, life itself shapes and regulates its environment to keep it habitable. For example, the oxygen content of the atmosphere and the salinity of the oceans have been held remarkably constant for eons, despite external perturbations. ________________________________________. They argue that these are emergent properties of the complex feedback loops between living organisms and their inorganic surroundings. The hypothesis remains controversial but has been profoundly influential in fostering an integrated, holistic view of Earth system science.
