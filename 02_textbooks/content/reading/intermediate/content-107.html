The "iron cage" is a sociological concept introduced by Max Weber to describe the increased rationalization inherent in modern capitalist society. Weber argued that the rational, bureaucratic structures that dominate modern life, while efficient, create a world where individuals are trapped in systems based on teleological efficiency, rational calculation, and control. The "separation of powers," a foundational principle of modern democratic governance, advocates for the division of governmental authority into distinct branches, each with separate and independent powers and areas of responsibility. Typically, these branches are the legislative (which makes laws), the executive (which enforces laws), and the judicial (which interprets laws). The primary purpose of this division is to prevent the concentration of power in a single entity and to protect against tyranny. ________________________________________. For instance, the legislature may pass a law, but the executive can veto it. The legislature, in turn, can override the veto. The judiciary can declare a law unconstitutional, but its judges are appointed by the executive and confirmed by the legislature. This intricate web of constraints ensures that the branches must work together and hold each other accountable.
