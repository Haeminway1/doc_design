The concept of "transhumanism" advocates for the use of science and technology to overcome fundamental human limitations, such as disease, aging, and even death. Proponents envision a future where technologies like genetic engineering, nanotechnology, and artificial intelligence allow humans to transition into a "posthuman" state with vastly enhanced intellectual, physical, and emotional capacities. Opponents, often called "bioconservatives," warn of a future with unprecedented social inequality, the loss of our common human identity, and the unforeseen dangers of tampering with our biological nature. The debate thus centers on the very definition and value of being human.

The "Dunning-Kruger effect" is a cognitive bias in which individuals with low ability in a particular domain overestimate their competence. Conversely, those with high ability tend to underestimate their relative competence, assuming that tasks easy for them are also easy for others. The core of the issue for the low-ability group is a deficit in metacognition. ________________________________________. This dual burden means they not only make mistakes but are also deprived of the very ability to recognize them. For instance, a poor chess player might fail to see the flaws in their strategy and thus believe they are more skilled than they are. Improving the skills of these individuals, however, also tends to improve their metacognitive ability, allowing them to make more accurate self-assessments.

  • (4) Therefore, external feedback has no impact on their self-perception.
  • (5) This cognitive bias is a sign of high intelligence and self-awareness.
