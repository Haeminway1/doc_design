The "interpellation," a concept from the political philosopher Louis Althusser, describes the process by which ideology constitutes individuals as subjects. Althusser argues that ideology is not just a set of false ideas but is a material practice embodied in social apparatuses like the family, school, and church. The process of interpellation is illustrated by his famous example of a police officer hailing someone on the street: "Hey, you there!" When the individual turns around, they have recognized themselves as the subject of the hail. In that moment, they are "recruited" into the ideological system. This recognition is not a free choice but a seemingly natural and obvious response. We are "always-already" subjects, constantly being interpellated by the ideologies that surround us. Thus, for Althusser, the category of the "subject," which seems to be the source of our freedom, is in fact ________________________________________.

  • (2) the effect of an ideological process of recognition
  • (3) a concept that exists only outside of social structures
  • (4) a force that can easily resist all forms of ideology
  • (5) a universal and unchanging feature of human nature

The "nomos," as used in the political theory of Carl Schmitt, refers to the fundamental act of appropriation and division of land that constitutes a political order. For Schmitt, every legal and political order is ultimately grounded in a concrete spatial ordering. The term goes beyond the modern concept of "law"; it is the primordial act of taking land, drawing lines, and establishing a distinction between inside and outside, friend and enemy. The *nomos* of the earth, in Schmitt's view, was historically based on the division of the globe by European powers during the age of discovery. This spatial order provided the foundation for international law for centuries. However, he argued that this old *nomos* was collapsing in the 20th century, leading to a global crisis. The struggle for a new *nomos* of the earth, a new fundamental spatial and political ordering, is therefore ________________________________________.

  • (4) the central political struggle of our time
  • (5) a process that leads to universal peace and harmony
