The "Ship of Theseus" is a thought experiment about the persistence of identity. It imagines a ship whose planks are gradually replaced one by one until no original planks remain. ① The question is whether this restored ship is still the same Ship of Theseus. ② To complicate matters, if the old planks were collected and reassembled, which of the two ships is the true one? ③ This paradox forces us to consider what constitutes identity: is it material composition, form, function, or history? ④ Philosophers have reached a clear and universal consensus on this problem, agreeing that identity resides solely in material continuity. ⑤ There is no easy answer, and different philosophical theories of identity, such as perdurantism and endurantism, offer competing solutions to the puzzle.

The "culture of honor" is a value system observed in some societies where people feel obligated to protect their reputation through aggression and violence.
