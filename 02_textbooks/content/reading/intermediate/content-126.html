( ④ ) It is concerned with ensuring the health, longevity, and productivity of the collective body of the populace through institutions like public health, demography, and social welfare. ( ⑤ ) This shift marks the entry of life into history, as human biological existence becomes a central object of political strategy.

In his *Poetics*, Aristotle offers a defense of tragic drama against Plato's charge that it merely excites base emotions and corrupts the soul. ( ① ) Aristotle agrees that tragedy arouses the emotions of pity and fear in the audience. ( ② ) However, he argues that the purpose of this arousal is not simply to indulge in emotion for its own sake. ( ③ ) Rather, through the carefully constructed plot and the downfall of a noble but flawed protagonist, tragedy provides a safe and structured context for the audience to experience these powerful emotions. ( ④ ) By witnessing the tragic events, the audience confronts fundamental aspects of the human condition. ( ⑤ ) Thus, for Aristotle, tragedy is not a corrupting influence but a valuable form of cognitive and emotional learning.
