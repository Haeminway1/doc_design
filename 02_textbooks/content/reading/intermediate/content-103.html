The "confirmation bias" is a pervasive cognitive bias that describes our tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information in a way that confirms or supports our pre-existing beliefs or hypotheses. It is a form of selective attention and information processing. Once we have formed a view, we tend to embrace information that supports it while ignoring, devaluing, or reinterpreting information that contradicts it. ________________________________________. For example, a person who believes a particular political party is corrupt will actively seek out news stories that highlight that party's scandals while dismissing stories about its successes as biased or irrelevant. This bias can lead to polarized beliefs and poor decision-making, as it prevents us from objectively evaluating evidence and considering alternative viewpoints. It is a major obstacle to critical thinking and open-mindedness.
