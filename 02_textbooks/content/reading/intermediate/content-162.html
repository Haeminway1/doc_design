The "uncanny valley" is a hypothesis in aesthetics and robotics regarding the emotional response of humans to non-human entities. The theory posits that as a robot's appearance becomes more human-like, our emotional response becomes increasingly positive, but only up to a point. ① When the entity becomes "almost human" but with subtle flaws, the response abruptly plunges into revulsion. ② This dip in affinity is the "uncanny valley." ③ The unsettling feeling is often attributed to the perception of a corpse or to a cognitive dissonance between the human-like appearance and the lack of a genuine human essence. ④ Therefore, designers of robots and animated characters should always aim to create entities that fall directly within this valley. ⑤ Once the appearance becomes virtually indistinguishable from a healthy human, the emotional response becomes positive again, climbing out of the valley.
