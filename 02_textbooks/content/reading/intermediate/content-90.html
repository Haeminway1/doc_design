In their collaborative work, philosophers Gilles Deleuze and Félix Guattari introduce the concept of the "Body without Organs" (BwO) as a crucial element in their critique of psychoanalysis and capitalism. The BwO is not a literal, organ-less body but rather a virtual plane of immanence, a surface upon which various intensities and desires flow before they are stratified and organized into a coherent organism or subject. Thus, the goal is not to eliminate all organization but to experiment with creating new, non-hierarchical connections on the surface of the BwO.

The "Euthyphro dilemma" is a classic problem in the philosophy of religion, originating in Plato's dialogue of the same name. In the dialogue, Socrates asks Euthyphro, "Is the pious loved by the gods because it is pious, or is it pious because it is loved by the gods?" This question presents a formidable challenge to any divine command theory of ethics, which grounds morality in God's will.
