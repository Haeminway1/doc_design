The "confirmation bias" is a pervasive cognitive bias that describes our tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information in a way that confirms our pre-existing beliefs. Once we have formed a view, we tend to embrace information that supports it while ignoring or devaluing information that contradicts it. This is not necessarily a conscious or malicious act; it is often a subconscious mental shortcut. This bias is a significant obstacle to critical thinking, as it insulates our beliefs from challenge and prevents us from objectively evaluating all available evidence.
