The "trolley problem" is a famous ethical thought experiment that pits two major moral frameworks against each other: utilitarianism and deontology. In the standard scenario, a runaway trolley is about to kill five people. (A)____________, you can pull a lever to divert it to another track, where it will kill one person. A utilitarian, who aims to maximize overall well-being, would likely pull the lever, as one death is better than five. A deontologist, (B)____________, might argue that pulling the lever constitutes an intentional act of killing, which is inherently wrong regardless of the consequences, thus violating a moral duty. The problem's power lies not in finding a "correct" answer but in revealing the deep-seated tensions in our moral intuitions.
Which of the following best completes (A) and (B)?
  • (1) For example / in addition
  • (2) However / on the other hand
  • (3) Therefore / similarly
  • (4) In fact / as a result
  • (5) In addition / consequently

The "paradigm shift," a concept introduced by historian of science Thomas Kuhn, describes a fundamental change in the basic concepts and experimental practices of a scientific discipline. Kuhn argued that science does not progress in a linear, cumulative fashion. Instead, it undergoes periodic revolutions. These anomalies accumulate until they trigger a crisis, leading to a scientific revolution where the old paradigm is overthrown and replaced by a new, incompatible one. The shift from Newtonian physics to Einsteinian relativity is a classic example. This new paradigm then redefines the field, its problems, and its standards of evidence.
