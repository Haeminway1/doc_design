The "Anthropocene" is a proposed geological epoch dating from the commencement of significant human impact on Earth's geology and ecosystems. The term highlights the idea that humanity has become a global geological force. ① Proponents point to evidence such as the dramatic increase in atmospheric carbon dioxide, widespread plastic pollution, and the global dispersal of artificial radionuclides from nuclear testing. ② These markers, they argue, will leave a permanent and globally synchronous signal in the rock strata. ③ There is, however, considerable debate about when the Anthropocene officially began. ④ Proposed starting dates range from the agricultural revolution thousands of years ago to the Industrial Revolution in the 18th century, or the "Great Acceleration" of the mid-20th century. ⑤ This lack of a single, clear starting point means that the concept has been entirely rejected by the scientific community.
