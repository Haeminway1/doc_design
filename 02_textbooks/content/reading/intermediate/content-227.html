The "picaresque" novel is a genre of prose fiction that originated in 16th-century Spain and is characterized by its realistic, often satirical, depiction of the adventures of a roguish hero of low social class. The protagonist, or *pícaro*, lives by his wits in a corrupt and stratified society. The narrative is typically autobiographical and told in the first person. (A) This first-person perspective provides an intimate, though not always reliable, view of the social world from the bottom up. (B) Unlike the hero of a romance, the *pícaro* does not mature into a virtuous character; his fortunes may rise and fall, but his essential character remains that of an outsider. (C) The plot is episodic, consisting of a series of loosely connected episodes or adventures as the *pícaro* moves from one master or situation to another. This structure reflects the instability and lack of direction in the protagonist's life.
