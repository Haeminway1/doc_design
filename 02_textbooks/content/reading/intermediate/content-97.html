The "fallacy of composition" is a logical error that arises when one infers that something is true of the whole from the fact that it is true of some part of the whole. In other words, it is the mistaken assumption that what is true for an individual is necessarily true for the group. A classic example can be found in economics. ________________________________________. If a single individual in a crowd stands on their tiptoes, they can see better. However, if everyone in the crowd stands on their tiptoes, no one's view is improved. Similarly, while it may be beneficial for one farmer to increase their crop yield, if all farmers do so, the resulting glut in the market could cause prices to collapse, harming all of them. This fallacy highlights the importance of considering systemic effects and the unintended consequences of collective action.
