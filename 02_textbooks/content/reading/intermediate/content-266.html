The "Fermi paradox" highlights the stark contradiction between the high probability of the existence of extraterrestrial civilizations and the utter lack of evidence for them. The argument is straightforward: our galaxy contains billions of stars, many of which are billions of years older than our sun. It seems statistically likely that intelligent life should have arisen on some of the planets orbiting these stars. If even a tiny fraction of these civilizations developed interstellar travel, the galaxy should have been completely colonized or at least visited by now. Yet, we have found no credible evidence of alien activity—no probes, no signals, no artifacts. ________________________________________. Proposed solutions range from the "rare Earth" hypothesis (life like ours is exceptionally uncommon) to the "great filter" theory (some barrier prevents civilizations from reaching an advanced stage).
