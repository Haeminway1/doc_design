The "impostor syndrome" is a psychological pattern in which an individual doubts their accomplishments and has a persistent, internalized fear of being exposed as a "fraud." Despite external evidence of their competence, such as academic degrees or professional success, those experiencing this syndrome remain convinced that they are undeserving and have fooled everyone. (A) This feeling is not a formal mental disorder but is a common experience, particularly among high-achieving individuals. (B) The cycle often involves over-preparing for a task out of fear of failure, then attributing any success to that extreme effort or to luck, rather than to their own ability. (C) This attribution reinforces the feeling of being an impostor, as the success is not seen as evidence of genuine competence. This pattern can lead to chronic anxiety, stress, and a reluctance to take on new challenges.
