The "general adaptation syndrome" (GAS) is a three-stage model describing the physiological response to stress, first identified by Hans Selye. The first stage is the "alarm reaction," where the body recognizes a stressor and activates the fight-or-flight response. ( ① ) This is followed by the second stage, "resistance," where the body adapts to the continued high level of stress. ( ② ) While the outward signs of alarm may disappear, the body remains on high alert, consuming resources at an accelerated rate. ( ③ ) This resistance, however, is not sustainable over the long term. ( ④ ) If the stressor continues beyond the body's capacity, the organism enters the third stage, "exhaustion." ( ⑤ ) In this final stage, the body's resources are depleted, leading to burnout, illness, and in extreme cases, death.
