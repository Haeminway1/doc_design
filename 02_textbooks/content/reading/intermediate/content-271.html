The "placebo effect" is the phenomenon in which a patient experiences real therapeutic benefits from an inert substance or sham treatment. This effect is not "all in the mind"; it can trigger measurable physiological changes. The patient's belief in the treatment is a key component. The context of the treatment, such as the perceived authority of the doctor and the ritual of administration, also plays a crucial role.
