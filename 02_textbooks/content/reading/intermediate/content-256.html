The "Overton window" is a model in political science that describes the range of ideas the public is willing to consider and accept. This "window" of acceptable policies can shift over time, moving an idea from being unthinkable, to radical, to acceptable, and finally to popular policy. The theory posits that most politicians are limited to supporting policies that fall within the Overton window if they wish to remain electorally viable. Therefore, the most effective way to change politics in the long run is not necessarily to elect different politicians, but to shift the window itself. This is a task often undertaken by social movements, activists, and think tanks. By consistently advocating for a "radical" idea, they can gradually make it seem less extreme, pulling the window in their direction and making their preferred policies seem like a reasonable compromise.
