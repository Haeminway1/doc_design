D. 키워드 영작 — 모범 답안 (전문)
111.We all know that a diet containing a variety of foods keeps our bodies healthy.
112.But sometimes we are not sure which foods are good for which body parts.
113.Nature, however, gives us a big clue.
114.Look at the following examples.
115.Each of these foods not only looks like a certain body part but is also good for that body part.
116.Slice open a tomato and compare it with the human heart.
117.You will see that they look similar.
118.They both have multiple hollow spaces and are red.
119.Researchers say that the chemicals that make tomatoes red are good for your heart and blood.
120.In addition, eating tomatoes can lower your risk of heart disease.
121.Look at the shape of a walnut. Do you notice anything?
122.Yes, it's very similar to the shape of the human brain!
123.A walnut is divided into two parts, just like the brain.
124.Walnuts also have wrinkles, which the brain has too.
125.Studies show that walnuts help our brains stay healthy and active.
126.They are also good for preventing Alzheimer's disease.
127.A slice of carrot looks like the human eye.
128.Carrots have some chemicals that can make vitamin A, which improves your vision.
129.It helps your eyes process light and send a clear image to the brain.
130.So if you want healthy eyes, eat carrots.
131.Cutting onions is not fun because it makes you cry.
132.But try slicing one anyway. You can see that the inside looks a little like a human cell.
133.Scientists say that onions contain vitamin B.
134.Vitamin B helps make new, healthy cells.
135.Now, let's move on to ginger. What body part comes to mind when you see it?
136.You may not like ginger's strong taste or smell.
137.but these come from a special chemical that prevents you from feeling sick and throwing up.
138.For this reason, ginger can be good for your stomach.
139.Isn't it amazing that some foods mirror the body parts that they are good for?
140.Find as many as you can and try to eat a variety of them.
