Lesson 2 Health Matters!
Talk It Out, Help Is Here — Part 1
본문

Host ①On today's show, we are going to learn about special park benches in Zimbabwe called Friendship Benches. ②We have two special guests. ③First, please welcome the founder of the Friendship Bench program, Dr. Dixon Chibanda. ④Can you tell us what inspired you to come up with the program?

Dr. Chibanda ⑤Sure. ⑥The idea came from an experience I had as a psychiatrist. ⑦One of my patients had become severely depressed. ⑧However, she couldn't get help because she was unable to afford the bus ride to the hospital. ⑨This shocked me, as I had never considered such a problem before. ⑩In fact, it is difficult for many people in Zimbabwe to pay for transportation and receive medical treatment. ⑪Furthermore, there has been a lack of psychiatrists in the country for many years. ⑫When the Friendship Bench program started in 2006, there were only eleven psychiatrists serving Zimbabwe's population of over twelve million people. ⑬I needed to find a way to bring psychiatry into the community.

Host ⑭Oh, so that was the beginning of it. ⑮Could you explain to us how the Friendship Bench program works?

Dr. Chibanda ⑯Certainly. ⑰The program was created as a way to provide accessible mental health services. ⑱It offers talk therapy sessions to people dealing with issues such as depression or anxiety. ⑲The sessions are free and are provided by respected elderly women from the community. ⑳These women, called "grandmothers," volunteer to sit with patients at the benches, listen to their stories, and provide them with emotional support.

문장별 해석
①
On today's show, we are going to learn about special park benches in Zimbabwe called Friendship Benches.
오늘 쇼에서, 우리는 짐바브웨에 있는 우정 벤치라고 불리는 특별한 공원 벤치에 대해 배울 것입니다.
②
We have two special guests.
두 명의 특별한 손님이 계십니다.
③
First, please welcome the founder of the Friendship Bench program, Dr. Dixon Chibanda.
먼저, 우정 벤치 프로그램의 창설자인 딕슨 치반다 박사를 환영해 주세요.
④
Can you tell us what inspired you to come up with the program?
이 프로그램을 만들게 된 영감에 대해 말씀해 주시겠어요?
⑤
Sure.
물론이죠.
⑥
The idea came from an experience I had as a psychiatrist.
이 아이디어는 제가 정신과 의사로서 겪었던 경험에서 나왔습니다.
⑦
One of my patients had become severely depressed.
제 환자 중 한 명이 심하게 우울해졌습니다.
⑧
However, she couldn't get help because she was unable to afford the bus ride to the hospital.
하지만, 그녀는 병원까지 버스 요금을 감당할 수 없어서 도움을 받을 수 없었습니다.
⑨
This shocked me, as I had never considered such a problem before.
이것은 저에게 충격이었는데, 이전에는 그런 문제를 생각해 본 적이 없었기 때문입니다.
⑩
In fact, it is difficult for many people in Zimbabwe to pay for transportation and receive medical treatment.
사실, 짐바브웨에서 많은 사람들이 교통비를 지불하고 치료를 받는 것이 어렵습니다.
⑪
Furthermore, there has been a lack of psychiatrists in the country for many years.
게다가, 수년간 그 나라에 정신과 의사가 부족했습니다.
⑫
When the Friendship Bench program started in 2006, there were only eleven psychiatrists serving Zimbabwe's population of over twelve million people.
2006년에 우정 벤치 프로그램이 시작되었을 때, 1200만 명이 넘는 짐바브웨 인구에 정신과 의사는 단 11명뿐이었습니다.
⑬
I needed to find a way to bring psychiatry into the community.
저는 정신의학을 지역사회로 가져올 방법을 찾아야 했습니다.
⑭
Oh, so that was the beginning of it.
아, 그렇게 시작된 거군요.
⑮
Could you explain to us how the Friendship Bench program works?
우정 벤치 프로그램이 어떻게 작동하는지 설명해 주시겠어요?
⑯
Certainly.
물론입니다.
⑰
The program was created as a way to provide accessible mental health services.
이 프로그램은 접근 가능한 정신 건강 서비스를 제공하는 방법으로 만들어졌습니다.
⑱
It offers talk therapy sessions to people dealing with issues such as depression or anxiety.
우울증이나 불안과 같은 문제를 겪고 있는 사람들에게 대화 치료 세션을 제공합니다.
⑲
The sessions are free and are provided by respected elderly women from the community.
세션은 무료이며, 지역사회에서 존경받는 노인 여성들이 제공합니다.
⑳
These women, called "grandmothers," volunteer to sit with patients at the benches, listen to their stories, and provide them with emotional support.
"할머니"라고 불리는 이 여성들은 벤치에서 환자들과 함께 앉아 그들의 이야기를 듣고 정서적 지지를 제공하는 자원봉사를 합니다.
