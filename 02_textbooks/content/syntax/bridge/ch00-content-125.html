    The Japanese art of Kintsugi, or "golden joinery," is the practice of repairing broken pottery with lacquer mixed with powdered gold, silver, or platinum. The philosophy behind it is that the breakage and repair are part of the object's history, rather than something to disguise. This makes the repaired piece ⓐ even more beautiful than the original. This tradition is rooted in the Japanese philosophy of wabi-sabi, ⓑ which embraces the flawed or imperfect. Instead of seeing the cracks as a weakness, Kintsugi highlights ⓒ it as a strength, a testament to the object's resilience. It is a powerful metaphor for embracing our own imperfections and ⓓ see beauty in our scars.
